Nice to meet you! I'm Stephen.

I'm in my last semester at UNC Chapel Hill studying biology, chemistry, and neuroscience. I'll be graduating in May, looking for a job as a research technician or something in healthcare. My goal is to become an eye doctor, ophthalmology, and to live in the Asheville area where I grew up.

The Roots of my Photography

I owe my adventurous and creative spirit to exploring the outdoors. There's nothing quite like hiking along the Blue Ridge Parkway in the springtime. I started doing wildlife photography because of this, and I also started a nature club at Tuscola High School in Waynesville, NC called the Naturalist Club. Since then, I've travelled to Florida, Nevada, New Orleans, and Quebec to do nature photography.
You can view some of my photos through this link.

Event Photography Sparks my Interest

What does all the nature have to do with portrait photography? It helped me to get out of my comfort zone, inspiring me to become more creative, and eventually to try doing photography for people. Besides, it's a whole lot easier taking pictures of people than it is of, let's say, a small bird soaring through the sky 40 feet away.

So, I started volunteering for the Folkmoot International Dance Festival back in 2018, and I've continued doing photography for them every year since. This helped me to become more creative with photography and to learn how to interact with clients. The festival is super awesome, with over 10 countries each year performing their folk dances and folk songs. Also, I will be donating 10% of the profit I make from event sessions to this wonderful organization. Here's their website if you would like to check them out.

Portrait Photography Takes Off

The summer after I started photography for Folkmoot, I became interested in capturing the personalities of people other than folk dancers. So, I learned more about how to use more advanced features on my camera, and I began following a lot more inspiring people on social media, such as Aaron Anderson, Joe McNally, and Marina Williams. My creativity was running wild, and I seized every opportunity to take portraits for people.

I was going into my sophomore year at UNC Chapel Hill, and I signed up for a class through the award-winning Hussman School of Journalism and Media called "MEJO 489 - Photojournalism, Lighting, and Business Techniques". This class helped me to expand my personal photographic vision and my professional portfolio by honing my newfound knowledge and skills of studio and location lighting, propping, and styling. Overall, I grew to become a whole lot more comfortable with portrait photography, and I gained the skills that I wanted to be as creative as possible.

Starting the Business

It's been more than 2 years since then, and I've been having a great time taking portraits. In every session, I aim to make it as fun as possible, while honing in on capturing your personality as well as your experiences in the most creative ways possible. I've even learned how to code websites!

My Creative Style

I love using colors to capture a certain mood in each image I take, whether that be the natural glow of the sun on your figure during golden hour, using a multitude of photography gels to portray a color combination, or using "lightsabers" to create vibrant images that fill up your environment.
